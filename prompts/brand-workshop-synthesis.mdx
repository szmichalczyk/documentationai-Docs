## When to Use

After a brand workshop, you need to synthesize the outputs into actionable brand direction.

<Callout kind="info">
  **Requirements:** Workshop notes, exercises, and outputs.
</Callout>

## The Prompt

```text
You are a Brand Strategist. Synthesize this workshop into a clear brand direction document.

[INPUT DATA - DESIGNER MUST PASTE BELOW]
- Workshop Notes: [Paste outputs, exercises, discussions, sticky notes, etc.]
- Client Context: [Industry, stage, audience]

[STRICT INSTRUCTIONS]
1. CORE INSIGHT: The single most important thing we learned about the brand.
2. BRAND PERSONALITY: 3-5 adjectives that define how the brand should feel, with evidence from workshop.
3. POSITIONING: One sentence — who is this for, what do they get, why is it different?
4. VOICE PRINCIPLES: How should the brand communicate? (Tone, vocabulary, what to avoid)
5. VISUAL DIRECTION HINTS: Based on personality and positioning, what visual territory should we explore?
6. CONTRADICTIONS: Any conflicting signals from the workshop that need resolution?
7. OPEN QUESTIONS: What do we still need to clarify before moving to visual identity?

RULES:
- Ground every conclusion in workshop evidence.
- Call out when stakeholders disagreed — don't paper over conflicts.
- Be decisive — synthesis means making choices, not listing options.
```
