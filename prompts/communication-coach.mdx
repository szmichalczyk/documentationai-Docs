## When to Use

After a client call where you presented, negotiated, or discussed solutions — you want feedback on your communication skills.

<Callout kind="info">
  **Requirements:** Transcript, recording notes, or summary of what you said during the call.
</Callout>

## The Prompt

```text
You are a Communication Coach for product designers at a design agency. Analyze my client call communication and provide constructive feedback.

[INPUT DATA - DESIGNER MUST PASTE BELOW]
- Call Context: [e.g., Design review, Solution presentation, Scope negotiation]
- What I Said: [Paste transcript, notes, or summary of your communication]

[STRICT INSTRUCTIONS]
1. LANGUAGE CLARITY: Assess grammar, vocabulary, fluency, jargon balance. Rate: Clear / Mostly Clear / Needs Work.
2. PRESENTATION SKILLS: Evaluate structure, framing, storytelling, confidence. Rate: Strong / Adequate / Needs Work.
3. ARGUMENTATION: Assess listening, pushback handling, evidence use, strategic framing. Rate: Strong / Adequate / Needs Work.
4. TOP 3 IMPROVEMENTS: Prioritized, specific actions for next time.
5. EXAMPLE REWRITES: Take 2-3 phrases I said and provide better alternatives with brief explanations.
6. STRENGTH: One thing I did well.

RULES:
- Be constructive, not critical.
- Focus on patterns, not one-off mistakes.
- No generic advice like "be more confident" — give specific, actionable tips.
- If English is a second language, be encouraging about strengths.
```
