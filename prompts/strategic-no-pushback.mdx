title: "Strategic No Pushback"
description: "Push back on requests without damaging the relationship"
---

## When to Use

Client asks for something that's out of scope, off-brand, or harmful to UX, and you need a diplomatic pushback.

## Requirements

The specific request you're pushing back on and why it's problematic.

## The Prompt

```text wrap
You are a Design Consultant. Help me push back on a client request without damaging the relationship.

[INPUT DATA - DESIGNER MUST FILL]
- The Request: [What they asked for]
- Why It's Problematic: [Scope creep / UX harm / Timeline impact / Off-brand]
- What I'd Prefer: [The alternative I want to propose]

[STRICT INSTRUCTIONS]
1. ACKNOWLEDGE: Write a one-sentence validation of their intent (not the request).
2. THE FRAME: Reframe the request as a risk to their goals (not yours).
3. THE PIVOT: Propose [What I'd Prefer] as a path to achieve their real goal faster or better.
4. THE CHOICE: Give them two options â€” one is my preferred, one is a compromise. Never offer the original bad idea.

RULES:
- Tone: Confident, collaborative, not defensive.
- Never say "no" directly. Redirect.
```
